How do i buy players?
To buy players you would need to register a team. It costs Â£10 for which you would be given 100,000 chips using which you can purchase players at an auction that would be held before start of the season. Named/Capped players will be available at a defined base price from which the bidding starts.
What do i win/get?
The money collected as part of registration of teams will be used towards prizes and awarded at the end of the season. There will be a variety of prizes to be won, including the team with most points. Any money that is left will be given to the club's charity
Who are named/capped players?
Based on the playing patterns in last couple of seasons, we have selected players who have played considerable amount of games. These players would be requested for consent to be made available as named/capped players in 6 categories namely Batsman, Bowler, All Rounder, Super Batsman, Super Bowler and Super All rounder. Each categorgy is entered into auction as a different player and set at different base prices for purchase individually.
What is super category?
All named/capped players in 6 categories namely Batsman, Bowler, All Rounder, Super Batsman, Super Bowler and Super All rounder. The categories define how you earn points.
  • For a player in batsman category you would earn points based on his batting but not bowling
  • For a player in bowler category you would earn points based on his bowling but no batting
  • For a player in All rounder category you would earn points on both batting and bowling
  • For a player in super category you would do the same, except that you would double all bowling and batting points
There are other ways you earn points see, how to earn points for full list.

And then a few twisters to decrease predictablity.
  • For Batsman & All Rounders who are either Not out or Run Out or LBW at the end of the innings they would earn 5 points (includes super category and points dont double; applicable only for named/capped players)
  • For Bowlers & All Rounders who bowled in an innings but have only bowled less than or equal to 50% of the max allowance will earn 5 points (includes super category and points dont double; applicable only for named/capped players)
  • 25% extra on all points earned on a winning game (applicable for both named/capped and unnamed players; rounded to base integer [i.e 10.9 will be just 10 not 11])
  • All points earned in tour will be doubled up (applicable for both named/capped and unnamed players)
  • All points in non stats match (ex: chairmans game etc) will be trippled (applicable for both named/capped and unnamed players)
At the end of the auction all chips that you have left will be converted into points (1 point for each 1000 left). Teams that donot have alteast 10 players will attact a penality fo 50 points per game
How are these rules arrived and will it not create a bias on who is defining them?
The rules are derived based on statistical analysis to create a balance. Data from the last 5 seasons has been used for this purpose. With respect to bias, the members who have been involved in defining the rules (introduced/changed for this season) will be forbidden to register a team (for this season)
